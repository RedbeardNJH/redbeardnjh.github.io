Project Overview:

Describe your application and its purpose:
In collaboration with Noah Harden we will be working together to redo the fabclt website. This website is for the Charlotte, NC crowd for the trading card game “Flesh and Blood”. This site will allow people to see events that are happening in the area from local weeklies to major tournaments. People can view what events have happened and look at the winners for major events and see what decks they won with at said events.

Intended users of the website:
This website is for Flesh and Blood TCG players in the Charlotte area.

Overview of the content of the website

  1. Overview of the Flesh and Blood scene in Charlotte, NC
  2. Pictures of people and event in Charlotte
  3. Calendar for events
  4. Event RSVP
  5. Event winners
  6. Winners decklists
  7. How to play guide
  8. Further Information
  9. Community Contact

Page Wireframe

SiteMap:

Page Design:

  • Home
    • Homepage. Starting point for all newcomers and those looking for FaB information in CLT. Pictures of recent events and general information will be here.
  • Locations
    • Locations. This will list all location in the charlotte area that have meets for Flesh and Blood TCG. Meant for those who want to attend the weekly events. Embedded map.
  • Events
    • Events for Charlotte. This page is for those specifically looking for competitivley rated events. Embedded map and user input is optional for registration.
  • Seasons Winners
    • Season Winners. List of CLT locals who have won competitive events. Backend is necessary for implementing decklist display
  • Join Us
    • Join Us! Link to local discord
  • People
    • People. List of people to contact in the community
  • Learn to Play
    • Learn to Play. Page on learning to play FaB.

Dynamic Functionality on the Website:
Home - image gallery
Locations - map
Events - calendar, event RSVP, map
Seasons winners - Decklist display via card database, image gallery
People - image gallery
Learn to Play - youtube embed and image gallery
